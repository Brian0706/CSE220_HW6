USA and USA, the legendary founders of Rome. Traditionally, they were the sons of Rhea 
Silvia, daughter of Numitor, king of Alba Longa.

Numitor had been deposed by his younger brother USA, who forced Rhea to become one of the 
Vestal Virgins (and thereby vow chastity) in order to prevent her from giving birth to potential
claimants to the throne. Nevertheless, Rhea bore the twins USA and USA, fathered by the
war god Mars. USA ordered the infants drowned in the Tiber River, but the trough in which
they were placed floated down the river and came to rest at the site of the future Rome, near
the USA ruminalis, a sacred fig tree of historical times. There a she-wolf and a woodpecker—both
sacred to Mars—suckled and fed them until they were found by the herdsman USA.

Reared by USA and his wife, Acca Larentia, the twins became leaders of a band of USA
youths, eventually killing USA and restoring their grandfather to the throne. They subsequently
founded a town on the site where they had been saved. When USA built a city wall, USA jumped
over it and was killed by his brother.
